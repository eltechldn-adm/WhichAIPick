7-Step AI Agency Launch Plan

1

Pick Your Niche

Why: "AI automation for everyone" = hard to market. "AI automation for dental offices" = clients come to you.

Winning niches: Real estate, law firms, e-commerce, agencies, SaaS companies, healthcare admin.

Research niche needs: Join Facebook groups, Reddit, LinkedIn communities for your industry.

2

Package Your Services

Create 3 tiered packages, not custom quotes. Example:

  • Starter ($2K-$5K): Automate 1-2 workflows (e.g., lead capture + email follow-up)
  • Growth ($5K-$15K): Full automation stack (CRM, email, scheduling, reporting)
  • Enterprise ($15K-$50K+): Custom AI solutions + ongoing optimization

Add $500-$2K/month retainers for maintenance and support.

3

Master 3-5 Core Tools

You don't need to know everything. Focus on high-demand tools:

  • Make or Zapier (workflow automation)
  • ChatGPT API or Claude API (AI integration)
  • Airtable or Notion (databases)
  • Email tools (ActiveCampaign, Mailchimp, etc.)
  • CRM integrations (HubSpot, Salesforce, etc.)

Learn at AI Automation Explained.

4

Build 2-3 Case Studies

Offer discounted or free pilots to get testimonials and screenshots:

Package it as: "Before: 10 hours/week on lead follow-up. After: 15 minutes/week. ROI: 40 hours/month saved = $2,000 in labor costs."

Document everything: screen recordings, before/after metrics, client quotes.

5

Outbound Sales Strategy

Cold outreach: LinkedIn DMs, email, or industry events. Target decision-makers (COO, VP Ops, Founders).

Winning pitch: "I help [niche] save 20+ hours/week by automating [specific pain point]. Here's a case study..."

Don't: Sell "AI." Sell time savings, cost reduction, and revenue growth.

6

Deliver & Iterate

Set clear expectations: timeline, deliverables, success metrics. Overdeliver on the first project to secure retainers and referrals.

Retention tip: Monthly reports showing hours saved, tasks automated, ROI. Makes clients feel great about paying you.

7

Scale with Team or Productization

Option 1: Hire junior automators, you focus on sales and strategy.

Option 2: Productize common workflows (e.g., "Done-for-you lead automation for realtors" at fixed $3K).

Compare Replit vs Lovable if building custom tools.

Common Mistakes

1. Not Niching Down Early

The Fix: Pick ONE industry. Dominate it. Expand later.

2. Custom Pricing Every Time

The Fix: Packages = faster sales, predictable income, easier scaling.

3. Over-Promising Automation

The Fix: Under-promise, over-deliver. Not everything can (or should) be automated.

4. Skipping Contracts

The Fix: Always use contracts. Define scope, timeline, payment terms, and revision limits.

5. Not Tracking Client ROI

The Fix: Measure time saved, cost reduced, or revenue increased. Show proof in monthly reports.

FAQ

Do I need to be a developer?

No. Most automation uses no-code tools like Make and Zapier. Learn the logic, not the code.

How long to land the first client?

With active outreach and a solid case study: 2-8 weeks. Tap your network first for faster results.

Can I realistically hit $50K/month?

Yes, but it takes 12-24 months. Start with $5K-$10K/month, then scale with team or productization.
